# Brand

Status: Template

Use this when writing onboarding, empty states, permission copy, App Store
metadata, screenshots, marketing pages, and product-facing docs.

## Brand Idea

`[App Name]` is `[product category]` you can talk to.

A person says or types what changed. The app turns that plain request into
real updates in its domain.

## Positioning

Lead with product category before AI benefits:

- what kind of app this is;
- what users can say or type;
- what the app changes;
- how users can see or recover from the result.

Do not position the starter clone as a general AI assistant, voice recorder, or
chat app unless that is the actual product.

## Voice

Copy should be plain, concrete, and calm.

Use:

- short sentences;
- real examples from the product domain;
- visible cause and effect;
- confidence without hype.

Avoid:

- AI buzzwords as the main claim;
- productivity cliches;
- abstract outcomes before orientation;
- permission or privacy explanations before the user is at that moment;
- implying features that are not implemented.

## Message Hierarchy

Use this order when introducing the product:

1. Category: what kind of app is this?
2. Input: what can the user say or type?
3. Action: what does the app update?
4. Trust: can the user see what changed, undo it, or recover?

## Copy Patterns

Adapt these to the clone's domain:

- Talk to your `[category]`.
- Type or say what changed.
- One request can create a real update.
- Say it once. See it saved.

Avoid generic claims such as:

- Your productivity, simplified.
- AI for your life.
- Complex requests, no problem.

## Required Boundaries

Brand brevity does not override required disclosures. Permission rationale,
server-side transcription, account deletion, Terms, Privacy, purchases, and
vendor tracking disclosures need clear copy where the surface requires them.
